Brendan Oliver IP Theft Investigation

Case ID: R-2025-018

Case Overview

This dashboard summarizes the formal digital forensic investigation into the intellectual property (IP) theft against Mr. Brendan Oliver. The investigation's goal was to identify the method of the breach, find the responsible party, and provide clear recommendations to prevent future incidents. This interactive report allows you to explore the investigation's process, the "whodunnit" analysis, and the final solutions.

The Incident

A data breach was confirmed. Sensitive intellectual property, "Project Titan," was stolen and an attempt was made to sell it to media outlets.

The Root Cause (Answer-First)

The breach was a **Third-Party Compromise**. An attacker used stolen credentials from a 2019 data breach to access the email account of Mr. Oliver's associate, Alex Chen, and found the IP that Mr. Oliver had emailed months prior.

Step 3: Preservation and Chain of Custody

Activity:

  • Created bit-for-bit, verified forensic images (E01 format) of all physical devices.
  • Hashed (SHA-256) all collected digital logs and stored them on a secure server.
  • Maintained a complete Chain of Custody log for all items.

Rationale:

To maintain the forensic integrity of the original evidence and ensure its admissibility in court (NIST, 2006; Fed. R. Evid. 901).

Root Cause Analysis

The core of the investigation was a Root Cause Analysis (RCA) to determine exactly how the breach occurred. We analyzed three potential causes. Click on each cause to see the analysis and its outcome. The chart illustrates the investigative effort, showing where our analysis focused.

Cause A: Insider Threat

Access from a friend, family member, or employee.

Cause B: Direct Cyber-Attack

Phishing or malware on Mr. Oliver's laptop.

Cause C: Third-Party Compromise

Breach via the email recipient, Alex Chen.

Status: ELIMINATED

Analysis:

A review of the laptop's unified logs and file system metadata (APFS) showed no unauthorized local logins or access to the IP files that correlated with the breach timeline.

Status: ELIMINATED

Analysis:

A full analysis of the laptop's forensic image (using tools like Autopsy and Volatility) found no evidence of malware, Remote Access Trojans (RATs), or suspicious processes. Router logs showed no unusual outbound data exfiltration.

Status: CONFIRMED ROOT CAUSE

Analysis:

The attacker used a **credential-stuffing attack** to gain unauthorized access to Alex Chen's email account, using credentials stolen from a previous, unrelated data breach. The attacker then searched his email, found the sensitive IP, and downloaded it.

Key Findings (The "Smoking Gun")

Finding 1: The Breach

Alex Chen’s G-Suite logs revealed successful logins from an IP address in **Romania** (`91.102.14.82`).

Finding 2: The Method

Mr. Chen's email was found in a 2019 "Finance App" data breach, exposing his password.

Recommendations

The root cause was a failure in basic security hygiene by a trusted third party. To prevent future incidents, we recommend the following solutions.

⚠ Immediate Solutions

  • **Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA):** Both Mr. Oliver and Mr. Chen have enabled MFA on all accounts.
  • **Change All Passwords:** All critical account passwords have been changed.

🛡 Long-Term Solutions

  • **Stop Using Email for IP Transfer:** Adopt a secure, zero-trust file-sharing platform that requires MFA and uses end-to-end encryption.
  • **Vet Third-Party Security:** The legal team must now vet the security practices (e.g., mandatory MFA) of any partner before sharing sensitive IP.
  • **Offer Security Audits:** Rize Investigations has offered to provide this "third-party security audit" service to secure the supply chain.
